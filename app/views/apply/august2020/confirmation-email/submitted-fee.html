What happens next

We will:

  • send you a confirmation email with the details on this page
  • send you a text message with your customer reference number
  • send you an information pack about the Child Maintenance Service
  • write to you with details of your application
  • contact you if we need any more information
  • try to contact the other parent using the information you've given
  • confirm the information we have is correct and collect any other information we need from them
  • work out how much child maintenance they need to pay and when to pay it

How long your application will take

The Department for Work and Pensions is taking steps to make sure it can continue to deliver essential services during the current coronavirus (COVID-19) outbreak while protecting its staff and customers’ wellbeing.

This will unfortunately affect some other services it provides including the Child Maintenance Service.

This means:

  • the other parent will become responsible for paying child maintenance within 7 days of us first contacting them
  • it might be up to 12 weeks before you receive your first child maintenance payment
Your application will take longer if we cannot contact the other parent.

If we need to contact you

If we have any questions about your application, we will call you from this number:

0800 171 2345

You might want to add this number to your contact list so you know it's us calling.

Your online child maintenance account

You'll need to sign in to your online child maintenance account using:

  • your customer reference number
  • your National Insurance number
  • your 7-digit personal identification number (PIN)

Contacting us about your application

ask that you do not contact us to request information about your application. Updates will be available when you sign in to your online child maintenance account.

If you need to tell us about a change in your circumstances, you can report this using your online account.
